<p><p>The molybdenum ore market is primarily divided into two types: primary molybdenite (MoS2) and associated ore. Primary molybdenite is extracted directly from molybdenum-rich deposits and is the main source for molybdenum production. In contrast, associated ore contains molybdenum as a byproduct of mining other metals, such as copper or tungsten. The demand for molybdenum, driven by its applications in steel and alloys, influences pricing and market dynamics for both primary and associated ore types.</p></p>
